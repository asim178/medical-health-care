DENGUE FEVER
Dengue fever is a mosquito-borne viral infection common in tropical regions including Pakistan.
Symptoms: High fever (104°F/40°C), severe headache, pain behind the eyes, muscle and joint pain, nausea, vomiting, swollen glands, rash appearing 2-5 days after fever onset.
Red flags (Severe Dengue): Abdominal pain, persistent vomiting, bleeding gums or nose, blood in urine/stool/vomit, fatigue/restlessness.
Tests: NS1 antigen test (early), Dengue IgM/IgG antibodies, CBC (platelet count drops in dengue).
Treatment: No specific antiviral. Rest, fluids, paracetamol. AVOID aspirin/ibuprofen.

TYPHOID FEVER
Typhoid is caused by Salmonella typhi, spread through contaminated food/water.
Symptoms: Gradual onset fever (rises daily), headache, abdominal pain, constipation or diarrhea, rose-colored spots on trunk, weakness.
Tests: Widal test, Blood culture (gold standard), CBC.
Treatment: Antibiotics (ceftriaxone, azithromycin). Hydration. Rest.

MALARIA
Caused by Plasmodium parasites transmitted by Anopheles mosquitoes.
Symptoms: Cyclical fever with chills and sweating, headache, muscle pain, nausea, vomiting. Severe: confusion, seizures, dark urine.
Tests: Rapid Diagnostic Test (RDT), Blood smear (thick/thin), PCR.
Treatment: Artemisinin-based combination therapy (ACT). Chloroquine for P. vivax in sensitive areas.

PNEUMONIA
Infection of the lungs caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi.
Symptoms: Cough (with phlegm), fever, chills, shortness of breath, chest pain when breathing/coughing.
Tests: Chest X-ray, sputum culture, CBC, blood cultures.
Treatment: Antibiotics (amoxicillin/azithromycin for community-acquired). Oxygen if SpO2 < 94%.

URINARY TRACT INFECTION (UTI)
Bacterial infection of the urinary tract.
Symptoms: Burning urination, frequent urge to urinate, cloudy or foul-smelling urine, pelvic pain (women), lower back pain.
Tests: Urine dipstick, urine culture and sensitivity.
Treatment: Antibiotics (nitrofurantoin, trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole). Increase fluid intake.

DIABETES (TYPE 2)
Chronic condition with high blood sugar due to insulin resistance.
Symptoms: Frequent urination, excessive thirst, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, blurred vision, slow healing wounds, tingling in hands/feet.
Tests: Fasting blood glucose, HbA1c, oral glucose tolerance test.
Management: Metformin (first line), lifestyle changes, blood sugar monitoring.

HYPERTENSION
Persistently high blood pressure (>130/80 mmHg).
Symptoms: Often asymptomatic. May cause headache, dizziness, nosebleeds, shortness of breath in severe cases.
Tests: Blood pressure measurement, kidney function tests, ECG, urine albumin.
Management: Lifestyle changes, ACE inhibitors, ARBs, beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers.

ANEMIA
Low hemoglobin/red blood cells causing reduced oxygen delivery.
Symptoms: Fatigue, weakness, pale skin, shortness of breath, dizziness, cold hands/feet, brittle nails.
Types: Iron-deficiency (most common), B12 deficiency, thalassemia.
Tests: CBC, serum ferritin, serum iron, B12 levels.
Treatment: Iron supplements, dietary changes, treat underlying cause.

GASTROENTERITIS
Inflammation of stomach/intestines, usually viral or bacterial.
Symptoms: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal cramps, low-grade fever.
Red flags: Blood in stool, high fever, severe dehydration, symptoms >72 hours.
Tests: Stool culture (if bacterial suspected), CBC.
Treatment: Oral rehydration salts (ORS), rest, bland diet. Antibiotics only if bacterial.

ASTHMA
Chronic inflammatory airway disease causing recurrent breathlessness.
Symptoms: Wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, cough (worse at night/early morning).
Triggers: Allergens, cold air, exercise, smoke, infections.
Tests: Spirometry, peak flow measurement.
Treatment: Short-acting beta-agonist (salbutamol) for relief. Inhaled corticosteroids for control.
